Quick start entry points to Ruby Programming

There's a sense of nostalgia seeing some of my earliest programming experiences listed at one startpoint, and it's hard not to revisit some for old time's sake.

My own personal favourite language is Ruby, because it helps when you need it, leaves you alone when you don't, and has a tolerant syntax. The best and most comprehensive guide to Ruby is the Pickaxe book.

Ideally beyond that, you'll want a framework suitable for the project, and for smaller scale projects, you don't want a big framework. For that reason, I like Sinatra and it's bigger brother Padrino.

There is nothing wrong with static sites, and there are plenty of tools to create them. Over a recent weekend I tried quite a lot of these, and concluded of those that didn't spectacularly crash, I could be most productive with nanoc.
