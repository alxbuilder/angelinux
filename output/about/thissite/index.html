Running a website from home


How this site was put together

This is my own website running on my own hardware from my own home. There is no reason why most people could not do exactly the same thing. Here is what you need, and what you need to do.

Hardware (a web server)

There are many home servers and media boxes sold to serve content so if you already have something like this, you may be able to skip this step. Assuming you haven't, you could visit the B3 Store like I did years ago.

Alternatively, there is no reason why you could not run a server on a Raspberry Pi. There you will find all you need to get started including downloads and tutorials. It can help, to buy something preconfigured to be sure you have everything you need.

A name for the site

Names (like special car number plates) come at a price, often very little. For a cost, I could have got http://angelinux.org/. My name provider offers various names like http://choose-a-name.hopto.org. This can be seen by tracing to this site.

It seems this activity is catching on, and there will soon be a dotFree domain. And there are other things you might want to do with your new powers!

Dynamic DNS Service

At it's simplest, DNS (Dynamic Name Service) is the reason why you can visit a website without knowing it's IP address, like the way you can dial from your contacts without remembering your friend's number.

Unfortunately, unlike that analogy, your friends phone number doesn't constantly change, like your IP address does. Unless you specifically ask and pay, providers lease and reassign those addresses not being used.

So, what you need is a way to associate an unchanging site name with a constantly changing address. That is what dynamic DNS services offer.

Monitoring

I use Pingdom to keep an eye on this site from the outside. I can recognise particular times from the history, such as when I moved house and was awaiting reconnection, and my horrendous downtimes after getting eFibre.
