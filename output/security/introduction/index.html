Things you should know...

Edward Snowden's accounts of mass surveillance have led many, myself included, to reassess security. To be called extremists (as that article does), for having these concerns, is a clear sign that things are happening that shouldn't.

To be honest, nothing that came out particularly surprised me. To be protected to an acceptable level, you really need to be using the Tor Browser Bundle, which raises a big red flag all by itself!

To make sure this section is as accurate as possible, I took on board The Tor Perspective , and based on that and other research, I hope this is accessible to most users.

For anyone who just wants a faster user experience, without worrying about the technical details should start by Blocking Google Analytics, used on a huge slew of sites.

  • Browser Fingerprints

    Your browser has a fingerprint. and if you're not using a stock browser, then you can assume it's as unique as your finger's print, and even if it's not, your device ID is.
    All the unique attributes of your device, language, timezone, software versions and plugins all combine to make browsers unique.

  • Don't addthis... don't do lots of things!

    Variations in setups that allow browser fingerprinting have the knock-on effect that each browser draws in a unique way on what's called the HTML5 canvas tag. Try the demonstration on the link, it's polite enough to ask your permission.
    The above link not only shows a demonstration of the technique in action, but also publishes a wall of shame of offending sites, the top three of which seem to be addthis, ligatus and plentyoffish.

    • All indications are that Addthis are unapologetic, and the code upon which they built is still in the wild. This is partularly nasty and underhand, and it has already been suggested that this technique be used to replace traditional cookies.


  • Persistent cookies

    Digital marketing companies don't like you deleting your cookies, and regularly use persistant cookies to allow trackers to recreate the cookies you deleted. These persistant cookies are also known as evercookies.
